NEWSLETTER TEMPLATE

Weekly value email structure and examples

Strategy

THE WEEKLY EMAIL

After the welcome sequence, subscribers receive a weekly email. This keeps Zeb top of mind and continues to build trust. The goal: be so valuable they look forward to opening it.

01

CONSISTENT

Same day, same time, every week. Predictability builds habit.

02

VALUABLE

Every email teaches something useful. No pure promotional emails.

03

PERSONAL

Written like a letter from a friend, not a corporate newsletter.

Format

THE TEMPLATE

NEWSLETTER STRUCTURE

Subject Line Curiosity-driven, benefit-focused (not clickbait)
Opening Personal hook or observation (2-3 sentences)
Main Content One tip, one story, or one lesson (the meat)
Action Item Something they can do today
Soft CTA Optional mention of program/service
Sign Off Personal closing from Zeb

Example

SAMPLE NEWSLETTER

Subject: The mistake that kills progress (and how to avoid it)

Hey [First Name],

Had an interesting conversation with a client this week.

She'd been doing great — showing up consistently, moving better, feeling stronger. Then she missed a week due to travel, and when she came back she said:

"I feel like I'm starting over. I probably lost all my progress."

Here's what I told her:

One week off doesn't erase weeks of work.

Your body doesn't reset to zero because you missed a few sessions. That's not how fitness works. What actually kills progress isn't taking a break — it's believing the break ruined everything and giving up.

The real danger is the story we tell ourselves. "I blew it, might as well start over later" becomes "I'll start next month" becomes "I'll start next year."

The solution? Shrink the restart.

When you fall off, don't wait for Monday. Don't wait for "the right time." Just do something small today. A 5-minute stretch. A walk around the block. Anything that proves to yourself that you're still in the game.

Progress isn't about perfection. It's about picking back up without drama.

Your action item: If you've been off track lately, do ONE small thing today. Right now, if you can. Don't make it complicated. Just move.

And if you're currently on a roll — keep going. Consistency compounds.

— Zeb

Week 2

STORY

Week 3

MINDSET

Week 4

RESOURCE

Guidelines

EMAIL BEST PRACTICES

DO

  • Send same day/time each week (Thursday 9 AM)
  • Write like you're talking to one person
  • Keep it scannable (short paragraphs)
  • Include one clear action item
  • Reply personally to responses
  • Use plain text styling (not heavy design)

DON'T

  • Send only when you have something to sell
  • Write novels (aim for 300-500 words)
  • Use excessive formatting or images
  • Include multiple CTAs (pick one)
  • Skip weeks without communication
  • Sound like a robot or corporate entity
