Email 1 — Day 0

WELCOME EMAIL

Subject: Your free guide is here (+ a quick hello)

Hey [First Name],

Welcome! I'm Zeb, and I'm genuinely glad you're here.

Here's the free guide you requested:
[BUTTON: Download Your Guide]

Quick intro: I'm a personal trainer in my 60s who specializes in helping adults 50+ get stronger, move better, and feel more confident in their bodies.

I don't believe in complicated programs or impossible goals. My philosophy is simple:
Structure. Motivation. Accountability.

Over the next few days, I'll send you some of my best tips — stuff I use with my clients every day. No fluff, no BS.

If you ever have questions, just hit reply. I read every email.

Talk soon,
Zeb

P.S. I'm in my 60s. If you think it's too late — watch me.

KEY ELEMENTS

  • Deliver the promised resource immediately
  • Keep intro brief — they don't know you yet
  • Set expectations for future emails
  • Include personal touch (P.S.)
  • Invite reply to build relationship

Email 2 — Day 2

VALUE EMAIL

Subject: The 60-second fix I give every new client

Hey [First Name],

When someone walks into my studio for the first time, I always start with the same thing.

Not a complicated assessment. Not a lecture about diet.

Just this: A 60-second hip mobility routine.

Why? Because tight hips are the root of so many problems for adults 50+. Back pain. Knee issues. Trouble getting up from chairs. Poor balance.

Here's the routine:

1. Hip circles (10 each direction)
Stand tall, hands on hips. Make slow circles with your hips like you're stirring a pot.

2. Standing hip flexor stretch (20 seconds each side)
Step one foot back, tuck your pelvis slightly, feel the stretch in your front hip.

3. Knee hugs (5 each side)
Standing, bring one knee to your chest, hug it gently, lower with control.

Do this every morning before coffee. Takes 60 seconds. Changes everything.

Try it tomorrow and let me know how it feels.

— Zeb

KEY ELEMENTS

  • Pure value — no selling
  • Specific, actionable tip
  • Explains the "why"
  • Easy to implement immediately
  • Invites engagement (reply)

Email 3 — Day 4

STORY EMAIL

Subject: Why I train people in my 60s

Hey [First Name],

People sometimes ask why I'm still doing this at my age. Why not retire? Take it easy?

Here's the honest answer:

Years ago, I watched too many people I care about decline. Lose their mobility. Lose their independence. Accept that getting older meant getting weaker.

I refused to accept that for myself. And I refuse to accept it for my clients.

The fitness industry is obsessed with 25-year-olds with perfect bodies. It ignores the people who actually need help the most — adults 50+ who just want to move without pain, play with their grandkids, and live independently.

That's why I do this. Not because I have something to prove, but because I've seen what's possible when someone commits to showing up.

I've watched 70-year-olds get off blood pressure medication. I've seen people who couldn't climb stairs start hiking again. I've helped grandparents get down on the floor to play with their grandkids — and get back up.

None of it requires extreme workouts or impossible diets. Just consistency. Just showing up.

If you're reading this, you've already shown up. That's the hardest part.

Keep going.

— Zeb

"There is nothing impossible to him who will try."
— Alexander The Great

KEY ELEMENTS

  • Personal, vulnerable story
  • Explains "why" behind the mission
  • Creates emotional connection
  • Social proof woven in naturally
  • Encouraging without being salesy

Email 4 — Day 7

SOFT OFFER EMAIL

Subject: What's next for you?

Hey [First Name],

Over the past week, I've shared my best quick tips and a bit about why I do what I do.

Now I want to ask: What would help you most right now?

Here are a few ways we could work together:

If you want to improve mobility on your own:
My 10-Minute Mobility program gives you a full 30-day plan of follow-along videos. Simple, effective, designed for 50+. Learn more here

If you're in the Redding, CA area:
I offer in-person training at my studio. New clients start with an assessment to see where you're at and create a plan. Book a session

If you just want to keep learning:
I'll keep sending weekly tips and updates. No pressure, no spam — just useful stuff to help you move better.

Whatever you choose, I'm glad you're here.

Questions? Just reply to this email. I answer everything personally.

— Zeb

KEY ELEMENTS

  • Multiple options (not one hard pitch)
  • Low-pressure language
  • "Just keep learning" option validates staying on list
  • Personal reply invitation
  • Links to offers without aggressive CTAs
