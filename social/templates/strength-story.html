Here's a number that should change how you think about exercise: After 50, you lose 1-2% of muscle mass every year. That might not sound like much. But compound it: - By 60: You've lost 10-20% of your muscle - By 70: You've lost 20-40% - By 80: You've lost 30-60% This is called sarcopenia. And it's responsible for: - Falls and fractures - Loss of independence - Decreased metabolism - Lower quality of life The good news? Strength training REVERSES this. You can build muscle at any age. I've seen clients in their 70s gain significant strength in just months. It's never too late to start. But the sooner you do, the more you keep. Questions about starting a strength program?
