Why your doctor's fitness advice is probably wrong: (No offense to doctors. But this needs to be said.) Medical school includes: → ~25 hours of nutrition education → ~0 hours of exercise programming → ~0 hours of behavior change Your doctor is trained to treat disease. Not optimize health. Common bad advice I hear: ❌ "Just walk more" → Walking alone won't build muscle or bone density ❌ "Lose weight before exercising" → Backwards. Exercise helps you lose weight. ❌ "Take it easy at your age" → This accelerates aging. You need to challenge yourself. ❌ "Avoid lifting heavy" → Heavy (relative) lifting prevents osteoporosis ❌ "Do more cardio for heart health" → Strength training is equally important for heart health What doctors should say: ✅ "Lift weights 2-3x per week" ✅ "Eat 1g protein per pound of ideal body weight" ✅ "Walk 8,000+ steps daily" ✅ "Prioritize sleep over exercise" ✅ "Get your vitamin D, B12, and hormone levels checked" Your doctor keeps you alive. A good coach helps you THRIVE. Respect both. But know the difference. #FitnessAdvice #HealthOptimization #FitnessOver50 #ThinkDifferent #ZebsPlatinumFitness #Longevity
