10,000 steps a day. You've heard it. But here's what nobody tells you: That number? It came from a 1960s Japanese marketing campaign for a pedometer. It wasn't based on science. Here's what the research actually shows: - Health benefits start around 4,000 steps - Maximum benefit peaks around 7,500-8,000 steps - More than that? Diminishing returns My recommendation for those 50+: Start where you are. Track your current average. Then add 500-1,000 steps per week. Walking is the most underrated exercise. It: - Improves heart health - Strengthens bones - Boosts mood - Aids digestion - Burns calories You don't need to hit 10K. You just need to walk more than you did yesterday.
