90% of people over 50 don't get enough protein. Here's how much you actually need: The RDA says 0.36g per pound of body weight. That's 54g for a 150lb person. THIS IS WRONG. That number prevents deficiency. It doesn't optimize health. For adults 50+, research shows you need: 0.7-1g per pound of IDEAL body weight That's 105-150g for most people. Why more protein after 50? "Anabolic resistance" Your muscles become LESS responsive to protein as you age. You need MORE protein to get the SAME muscle-building effect as a 30-year-old. What happens without enough protein: → Muscle loss (1-2% per year) → Slower metabolism → Weaker bones → Slower recovery → More body fat → Frailty The simple protocol: Breakfast: 30-40g protein → Eggs + Greek yogurt → Or protein shake Lunch: 30-40g protein → Chicken, fish, or meat → With vegetables Dinner: 30-40g protein → Same as lunch → Don't skimp here Quick protein wins: • 4 eggs = 24g • Greek yogurt cup = 15-20g • Chicken breast = 35g • Protein shake = 25-30g • Cottage cheese = 25g Track your protein for one week.
