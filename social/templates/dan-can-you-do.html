Most 60-year-olds can't do this. Can you? The challenge: Get down on the floor. Now stand back up. Without using your hands, knees, or any support. This is called the Sitting-Rising Test (SRT). Why it matters: A study of 2,000+ adults found: Those who needed to use hands/knees to stand had a 5-6x higher risk of dying in the next 6 years. It tests: → Leg strength → Core stability → Balance → Flexibility → Motor coordination Scoring: Start with 10 points (5 for sitting, 5 for rising) Subtract 1 point for: • Each hand used for support • Each knee used for support • Each loss of balance Scores 8-10: Excellent Scores 6-7: Good Scores 3-5: Fair Scores 0-2: Poor (high risk) If you scored below 8: This is your wake-up call. The good news? This is 100% trainable. Start with: → Goblet squats → Step-ups → Single leg balance → Core work Test yourself again in 30 days. Drop your score in the comments.
